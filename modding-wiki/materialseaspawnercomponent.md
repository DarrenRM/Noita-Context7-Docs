# MaterialSeaSpawnerComponent

This documentation covers the `MaterialSeaSpawnerComponent` in Noita, a crucial component for defining and controlling the behavior of "seas" of materials within the game. Understanding this component is essential for modders looking to create custom biomes, unique environmental effects, or alter existing liquid behaviors.

## Overview

The `MaterialSeaSpawnerComponent` is responsible for spawning and managing large bodies of specific materials, often referred to as "seas" or "lakes." This component dictates which materials can appear in these seas, how they are generated, and their physical properties within the game world. Modders can leverage this component to introduce new liquid types, modify the composition of existing seas, or create dynamic environmental hazards.

## Component Properties

The `MaterialSeaSpawnerComponent` has several properties that can be configured in your mod's XML files. These properties allow for fine-grained control over the sea's behavior.

### `material_spawn_probability`

This property defines the probability of a specific material being chosen for spawning within the sea. It's typically used in conjunction with a list of materials to create a mixed sea.

### `material_spawn_radius`

Determines the radius around a spawn point where materials will be considered for spawning.

### `material_spawn_amount`

Controls the quantity of a specific material to spawn.

### `material_spawn_min_amount`

The minimum amount of a material that will be spawned.

### `material_spawn_max_amount`

The maximum amount of a material that will be spawned.

### `material_spawn_delay`

The delay in seconds before a material is spawned.

### `material_spawn_delay_min`

The minimum delay in seconds before a material is spawned.

### `material_spawn_delay_max`

The maximum delay in seconds before a material is spawned.

### `material_spawn_force`

The force applied to spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_force_min`

The minimum force applied to spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_force_max`

The maximum force applied to spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_velocity`

The initial velocity of spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_velocity_min`

The minimum initial velocity of spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_velocity_max`

The maximum initial velocity of spawned materials.

### `material_spawn_random_velocity`

If true, the spawned material's velocity will be randomized within the defined min/max ranges.
