## Understanding Enemy Data for Modding

The information presented in the table above is crucial for several aspects of Noita modding:

*   **Enemy Identification:** The `id` field is the unique identifier for each enemy, which is essential for referencing them in Lua scripts and XML files.
*   **Balancing and Difficulty:** Health values, bleed types, and spawn locations directly influence the difficulty of encounters. Modders can adjust these to fine-tune the challenge.
*   **AI and Behavior:** While not explicitly detailed in this table, the `faction` and `spawnLocation` can hint at enemy behaviors and their typical environments, which can be further manipulated through scripting.
*   **Loot and Drops:** The `corpse` field indicates what material the enemy drops upon death, which can be relevant for crafting or other game mechanics.

### Key Data Fields Explained

*   **In-game Name:** The name displayed for the enemy within the game.
*   **Alias:** An alternative or descriptive name for the enemy.
*   **id:** The internal identifier used by the game engine. This is vital for scripting.
*   **Health:** The enemy's hit points. Values may vary based on biome or specific variants.
*   **Bleeds:** The type of liquid or material the enemy bleeds when damaged. This can interact with other game mechanics and liquids.
*   **Corpse:** The material that the enemy's body transforms into upon death.
*   **Spawn Locations:** The biomes or areas where the enemy is typically found.
*   **Faction:** The faction the enemy belongs to, which can affect AI interactions and relationships with other entities.
